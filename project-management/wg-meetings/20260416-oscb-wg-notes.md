## Notes

Africa, as a continent, faces inequality in the quality of research for infectious diseases

  * 6 of the top ten causes of death in LIC are still infectious diseases.
  * Low-income countries produce 5% of the world's research: More research comes out from the global north than the global south.
  * Less than 15% of drugs currently in development target infectious diseases.
  
  
* Tools being built by the Ersilia hub to curb this:
  * The Ersilia model hub.
     * The models contain:
         * Annotation models
         * Representation models
         * Sampling models
         * Property Prediction or calculation models
         * Featurization
         * Activity prediction models
         * Projection models
         * Generation models
* Challenge with AI Models
   * No common format: Every lab builds models differently. Sharing a model means the next person must recreate your entire setup from scratch
   * Hard to reproduce: Without a standard, the same model may give different results in different environments, making the science hard to validate
   * Slow to deploy: Getting a model from a laptop to a shared server requires custom engineering every single time
* How to build models with your own data:
   * Tools like ZariaChem enable researchers to build molecular prediction models                                          
* Why does it matter to scientists                              
   * Less manual setup: Instead of stitching together preprocessing, descriptors, modeling, and reporting yourself, the workflow runs those steps in sequence.                                                  
   * More reproducible runs: The same steps are applied every time, which makes it easier to compare experiments and explain how a model was produced.                                                  
   * Easier handoff and reuse: A trained model can be kept and later used to score new molecules, which is ideal for screening or follow-up batches.                       
* Current Situation of Open Source Across Africa :
   * < 2% of global open-source contributions
       * Underrepresentation of datasets across the continent, meaning most of the tools are bias towards the continent with minimal local context
   * High consumption but low ownership 
   * Very few large-scale open datasets
       * Either non-existent or not digitalised.
   * Open-source communities are small, localized, and poorly interconnected 
   * Brain drain                                                          
* What can be done? 
   * Increase funding:
       * Even at elementary levels, more students have access to computers and digital literacy at an early age.     
       * Open health datasets
       * Invest in training programs
       * Strengthen digital infrastructure
       * Promote African-led open-source projects
       * Foster collaboration
           * Aids faster growth and avoids duplication
   * Why it matters
       * Allows more institutions to produce scientific outputs
       * Speeds up innovation by widening access to AI
       * Leads to more effective health solutions
       * Provides locally tailored solutions to regional challenges
