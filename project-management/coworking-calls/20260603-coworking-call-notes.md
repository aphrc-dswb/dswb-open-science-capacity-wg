## Notes

Journal Club Material:

    

O’Sullivan, J.W., Palepu, A., Saab, K. et al. A large language model for complex cardiology care. Nat Med 32, 616–623 (2026). [https://doi.org/10.1038/s41591-025-04190-9](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41591-025-04190-9)

   * Why was the paper chosen:
       * ﻿﻿First RCT of an LLM in subspecialty cardiology care
       * ﻿﻿Real-world patient data, not synthetic vignettes
       * ﻿﻿Open-source dataset, reproducible by the community
       * ﻿﻿Directly addresses the generalist-subspecialist gap
           * 18% of SSA countries have no registered cardiologist
           * <5 physicians per 10,000 people in most SSA countries
           * 60% of HCM patients undiagnosed in the US, estimates worse globally
   * Authors' stated implications
       * Authors' conclusion: LLMs can assist generalists in subspecialty-level complex
           * ﻿﻿First RCT-level evidence that LLMs can improve clinical care quality in a subspecialty domain
           * ﻿﻿Open-source dataset and validated 10-domain evaluation rubric available for community replication
           * ﻿﻿Demonstrates feasibility of the RCT as the evidence standard for LLMs in clinical medicine
           * ﻿﻿Authors explicitly state 'premature to deploy LLMs autonomously', cardiologist oversight remains essential
